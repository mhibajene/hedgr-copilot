# Hedgr Default Allocation Policy

Version: 1.0

Status: Binding

Scope: Retail Layer (MVP and beyond)

---

## Purpose

To operationalize Hedgr’s doctrine of Capital Preservation Above All by defining how user capital is allocated by default.

This policy encodes treasury discipline at the structural level.

---

## Article I — Structural Split (Option B Model)

All retail deposits are automatically allocated into two components:

1. Stability Buffer
2. Conservative Yield Allocation

This split is mandatory and not optional at the base layer.

---

## Article II — Stability Buffer

### Definition

The Stability Buffer is a liquidity-first allocation held to:

- Absorb volatility
- Ensure withdrawal reliability
- Reduce systemic exposure risk
- Preserve principal in stress conditions

### Minimum Threshold

- Default target: 60–70% of deposited capital
- Absolute minimum threshold: 50%
- Buffer percentage may increase during stress conditions

### Characteristics

- High liquidity
- Minimal protocol risk
- No leverage
- No experimental exposure
- Rapid withdrawal priority

The Stability Buffer is not idle capital.
It is active risk management.

---

## Article III — Conservative Yield Allocation

### Definition

The Yield Allocation is a capped deployment of capital to conservative, vetted protocols.

### Exposure Controls

- No single protocol may exceed predefined exposure caps.
- Diversification across protocols is mandatory.
- Yield exposure is interruptible.
- Allocation may be reduced automatically during stress.

### Yield Philosophy

Yield is:

- Enhancement, not entitlement
- Secondary to preservation
- Suspended if principal risk increases

---

## Article IV — Crisis Adjustment Logic

Under predefined stress triggers:

- Yield Allocation % may be reduced
- Stability Buffer % may be increased
- Yield may be temporarily suspended
- Users are notified with transparent explanation

Principal protection overrides return continuity.

---

## Article V — User Interaction Model

Users may:

- View allocation breakdown
- View buffer percentage
- View exposure caps
- View stress-response rules

Users may not:

- Reduce buffer below constitutional minimum
- Override exposure caps
- Opt into leverage or speculative yield in retail layer

---

## Article VI — Governance & Enforcement

Any change to:

- Buffer minimum
- Exposure cap structure
- Yield deployment rules

Requires:

- ADR entry
- Risk classification
- Liquidity impact analysis
- Charter alignment review
- HedgrOps approval

Tier 3 breaches are blocked unless explicitly overridden with documented accountability.

---

## Closing Principle

Predictable outcomes are achieved not by maximizing yield,
but by controlling exposure and preserving liquidity.

The Default Allocation Policy encodes this discipline structurally.
