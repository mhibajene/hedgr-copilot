

# Hedgr Product Surfaces

Version: 1.0
Status: Canonical Product Surface Definition

---

# 1. Overview

Hedgr is designed as a **financial stability operating system** rather than a single application.

To support different user segments and financial workflows, the platform exposes three primary product surfaces:

1. **Hedgr Wallet** – Stability wallet for individuals
2. **Hedgr Pro** – Treasury management platform for SMEs and institutions
3. **Hedgr APIs** – Infrastructure layer for fintech platforms and partners

All three surfaces are powered by the same underlying system:

- Hedgr Stability Engine
- Yield Routing Engine
- Asset custody infrastructure
- Payment rails

These surfaces represent different **interfaces into the Hedgr stability system**.

---

# 2. Hedgr Wallet

## Definition

Hedgr Wallet is the **consumer-facing stability wallet** designed for individuals operating in volatile currency environments.

The wallet allows users to preserve and manage value through stable digital assets while abstracting the complexity of financial infrastructure.

---

## Primary Users

The wallet is designed for:

- freelancers receiving international payments
- remote workers
- digital entrepreneurs
- mobile-first savers

These users typically face:

- currency volatility
- limited access to global financial tools
- banking friction

---

## Core Capabilities

Key capabilities of Hedgr Wallet include:

### Stability Savings

Users convert local currency into stable digital assets to protect purchasing power.

### Yield Generation

The Stability Engine allocates a portion of assets into conservative yield strategies.

### FX Awareness

Users can monitor currency movements and understand how local currency volatility affects their savings.

### AI Copilot Guidance

The Hedgr Copilot provides:

- financial education
- savings nudges
- yield explanations

---

## Product Objective

The goal of Hedgr Wallet is to become the **default stability wallet for users in volatile economies**.

---

# 3. Hedgr Pro

## Definition

Hedgr Pro is the **treasury management platform** built for businesses and professional users.

It provides tools that allow SMEs and institutions to manage treasury balances exposed to currency volatility.

---

## Primary Users

Hedgr Pro targets:

- SMEs operating across borders
- startups holding stablecoin treasury
- global freelancers operating as small businesses
- financial operators managing digital asset balances

---

## Core Capabilities

### Treasury Allocation

Businesses can allocate capital across stability, yield, and liquidity bands.

### Multi-Account Management

Organizations can manage multiple treasury accounts.

### Treasury Visibility

Businesses gain visibility into their stability allocations and yield performance.

### Institutional Controls

Future features may include:

- role-based access
- treasury policies
- risk exposure controls

---

## Product Objective

The goal of Hedgr Pro is to provide a **modern treasury system for businesses operating in volatile financial environments**.

---

# 4. Hedgr APIs

## Definition

Hedgr APIs expose the underlying infrastructure of the Hedgr system to external platforms.

This allows fintech companies and platforms to integrate Hedgr stability functionality into their own products.

---

## Primary Users

Hedgr APIs are designed for:

- fintech platforms
- neobanks
- payment processors
- digital wallets

These platforms can embed stability functionality without building the infrastructure themselves.

---

## Core Capabilities

### Stability Engine Access

Platforms can allocate capital through the Hedgr Stability Engine.

### Treasury Infrastructure

Partners can use Hedgr's infrastructure for stable asset treasury management.

### Stable Asset Conversion

APIs enable conversion between local currencies and stable digital assets.

### Yield Infrastructure

Partners may access yield routing infrastructure through controlled API endpoints.

---

## Product Objective

The goal of Hedgr APIs is to position Hedgr as **financial stability infrastructure for fintech ecosystems**.

---

# 5. Shared System Core

Although Hedgr exposes multiple product surfaces, all surfaces share the same underlying system components.

These include:

- Hedgr Stability Engine
- Yield Routing Engine
- Wallet Infrastructure
- Payment Rails
- AI Copilot

This shared architecture ensures that improvements to the system benefit all product surfaces.

---

# 6. Long-Term Platform Vision

Over time, Hedgr aims to evolve from a single application into a **financial stability infrastructure layer** for global users and fintech platforms.

The three product surfaces represent the foundation of that architecture:

Hedgr Wallet → consumer stability

Hedgr Pro → institutional treasury

Hedgr APIs → fintech infrastructure

Together these surfaces create a unified system that enables individuals, businesses, and platforms to manage financial volatility through programmable financial infrastructure.
