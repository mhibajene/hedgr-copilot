# Hedgr — Building Verifiable DeFi Savings for Emerging Markets

## Executive Summary

Emerging economies across Sub-Saharan Africa face chronic currency volatility, double-digit inflation, and limited access to dependable savings instruments. Freelancers, SME owners and everyday savers watch their hard-earned income lose 10–30% of its purchasing power each year, while mobile money wallets — although widely used — offer no interest or protection against depreciation.

Traditional banks rarely pay rates that outpace inflation.

Hedgr is a decentralized, AI-powered savings wallet designed to address these structural gaps.

Built on four pillars — **trust, growth, accessibility, and user-centricity** — the platform enables users in volatile economies to:

- Convert local currency into USD-stablecoins
- Earn risk-adjusted DeFi yield
- Verify the safety of their deposits via zero-knowledge proofs

Our **AI Copilot** delivers personalized financial education, FX insights and savings nudges through a mobile app and WhatsApp bot, making sophisticated finance accessible to anyone with a phone.

For investors, Hedgr offers a scalable platform that bridges traditional finance and decentralized value creation. Yield routing, modular architecture, and audited smart contracts create a compliance-first infrastructure with multiple revenue streams:

- yield spreads
- transaction fees
- B2B licensing

By partnering with mobile money operators, banks and DeFi protocols, Hedgr is positioned to capture the growing demand for stable, verifiable savings in Africa and beyond.

# Introduction: The African Financial Landscape & Hedgr's Vision

## Problem Statement — Currency Volatility and Limited Yield

Sub-Saharan Africa’s economies are buffeted by persistent inflation and currency depreciation.

In Zambia, consumer price inflation reached ~16.8% in February 2025 due to soaring food prices and a more than **30% depreciation of the kwacha in 2024**.

Across the continent, many currencies lose **10–30% of their value annually**, and some (like Nigeria’s Naira) have fallen by **over 100% against the US dollar since 2024**.

Despite this instability, savings options remain limited.

Mobile money wallets such as:

- M-PESA  
- MTN MoMo  

are widely used but do **not pay interest** and often charge transaction fees.

Traditional bank accounts rarely provide yields that compensate for inflation.

Accessing decentralized finance (DeFi) is also difficult:

- most users lack technical skills
- private key management is complex
- protocol risk is difficult to evaluate
