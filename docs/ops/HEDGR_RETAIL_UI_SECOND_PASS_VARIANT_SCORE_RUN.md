## 5. Comparative readout

### 5.1 Which variant best reduces mid-layer congestion?

**Variant C1 — Lower trust-detail emphasis** reduces congestion most aggressively, but at a noticeable cost to visible structural truth.

**Variant C3 — Actions before activity** reduces congestion more responsibly by clarifying order rather than removing too much trust detail.

### 5.2 Which variant best preserves Concept C as protected money?

**Variant C3 — Actions before activity**

This variant keeps the product money-first while preserving enough visible structure to remain unmistakably Hedgr.

### 5.3 Which variant introduces the most scope-creep risk?

**Variant C2 — Activity before actions**

This variant most strongly risks reclassifying the screen toward dashboard behavior or broader Activity-surface expectations.

### 5.4 Which variant most directly improves the disclosure problem?

**Variant C4 — Alternate disclosure label**

This is the clearest semantic intervention, but it does not resolve the whole structural problem by itself.

### 5.5 Which variant is the strongest current refinement of Concept C?

**Variant C3 — Actions before activity**

This is currently the strongest refinement centerline for continued paper critique.

## 6. Main strengths and doctrine risks

### 6.1 Variant C1 — Lower trust-detail emphasis

**Main strength**  
Best reduction of clutter and strongest money-feel simplicity inside the C-family.

**Main doctrine risk**  
Trust detail may become too recessive, weakening Hedgr’s structural truth.

**Main unresolved question**  
How light can above-the-fold trust detail become before the screen effectively collapses into Concept A behavior?

### 6.2 Variant C2 — Activity before actions

**Main strength**  
Best test of early financial orientation and “what changed?” legibility.

**Main doctrine risk**  
Strongest risk of dashboard drift and Activity scope creep.

**Main unresolved question**  
Can Activity ever come first without changing the product-class impression?

### 6.3 Variant C3 — Actions before activity

**Main strength**  
Best balance of money-first clarity, bounded Activity, and visible structural truth.

**Main doctrine risk**  
Actions, Activity, and Allocation / Trust Detail can still compete if separation is not disciplined.

**Main unresolved question**  
What is the minimum separation needed between Actions and Activity to keep the upper reading path clean?

### 6.4 Variant C4 — Alternate disclosure label

**Main strength**  
Strongest direct response to the disclosure clarity problem with minimal structural disruption.

**Main doctrine risk**  
Naming may improve while the deeper disclosure role remains only partially resolved.

**Main unresolved question**  
Is the disclosure issue primarily semantic, or does the layer still need structural narrowing?

## 8. Interpretation of the result

The current second-pass outcome suggests:

- **C3** is the strongest structural refinement of the Concept C centerline
- **C4** is the strongest semantic refinement and may be complementary rather than competing
- **C1** is useful as a lower-bound simplification test, but likely too reductive as the centerline
- **C2** remains valuable mainly as an edge probe for orientation, not as the likely refinement path

The practical reading is not that C3 is final.

The practical reading is:

**Variant C3 is currently the strongest refinement baseline for continued paper critique because it best reduces ambiguity without weakening Hedgr’s protected money posture.**
