## 1. Purpose

This document compares a **small bounded set** of status-copy treatments for the **Stability Status** layer on the calm-money retail dashboard baseline (C3 centerline).

The UI is already in the right **emotional lane**: calm, money-first, and structurally promising. The open question is **bounded meaning**—whether status reads as a legible trust signal or as decorative atmosphere—not louder messaging, broader information architecture, or new surfaces.

All wording and examples below are **critique-only prompts** for internal comparison. **Nothing here is approved final UX copy**, implementation authorization, or sequencing authority.

## 4. Why this comparison is needed

Stability Status is becoming **one of the defining surfaces** of Hedgr: it sits immediately after portfolio value and frames protection posture. Without a **small amount of bounded explanatory consequence**, the layer can still read as **too atmospheric**—mood without interpretive weight.

This comparison tightens the status-meaning test note into a **side-by-side copy lens** so reviewers can judge which direction best answers:

**Which status-copy direction best improves meaning without becoming louder, more operational, or more system-authored?**

It is intended to:

- improve **interpretability** in one reading pass
- preserve **calmness** and money-first hierarchy
- avoid **hidden-engine implication** (triggers, undisclosed rules, or “live system” theater)

It is **not** intended to:

- expose **richer system logic** or a visible state machine
- create a **full retail state vocabulary** or finalize shipping copy
- substitute for **legal, trust, or doctrine review** of any candidate line

## 6. Comparative readout criteria

Judge variants consistently against:

- **calmness** — no louder or more urgent than the baseline screen
- **immediate interpretability** — understood in one pass without secondary explanation
- **trustworthiness** — honest, non-promissory, aligned with read-only informational truth
- **non-theatrical tone** — no dashboard theater, fake precision, or performance of hidden sophistication
- **money-first fit** — status supports the portfolio anchor; it does not compete with it
- **avoidance of hidden-engine implication** — no suggestion of secret rules, guaranteed outcomes, or deterministic behavior
- **fit with Hedgr’s protected-money identity** — unmistakably Hedgr, not generic fintech wellness or yield hype

## 7. Comparative assessment

### 7.1 Direction A — Label only

**Preserves** maximum calm and minimal reading load; keeps the layer from feeling operational or dense.

**Lacks** bounded explanatory support—strongest risk of “atmosphere without consequence” relative to B and C.

**Remains useful** as a **calmness control** and regression check: if B or C cannot beat A on trust and clarity, they should not advance.

### 7.2 Direction B — Label + bounded supporting line

**Likely strongest current comparison baseline** — it is the direction repeatedly surfaced in pass resolution and meaning notes as the primary live test: one short line that states posture in plain language without exposing the engine.

**Improves** interpretability and trust legibility while staying subordinate to portfolio value and consistent with informational framing.

**Still needs caution** around implied guarantees (“protection range” must stay honest to what the read-only surface can support), length, and any drift toward **advisory** or **directive** subtext—those boundaries stay translate-before-use, not assumed copy.

### 7.3 Direction C — Label + slightly stronger explanatory line

**Useful as an upper-bound test** — stress-tests how much meaning one line can carry before density or jargon rises.

**Risks** sounding more **system-authored**, more like internal architecture (“structure”), or heavier to scan—closer to machine narration if not disciplined.

**Should remain critique-only** unless a later governed pass explicitly narrows semantics, honesty bounds, and disclosure alignment; it is not a default centerline on wording alone.

## 11. Working conclusion

Stability Status should become **more meaningful in a bounded way**: enough interpretive consequence to deepen trust, not enough to impersonate the engine or invite operational reading.

Keep clear separation between **interpretation** (what the user understands about posture), **engine exposure** (what must not be implied at this layer), and **advisory** (what must not be smuggled in as neutral copy).

**Status copy should deepen trust through calm interpretation, not through visible machinery.**
