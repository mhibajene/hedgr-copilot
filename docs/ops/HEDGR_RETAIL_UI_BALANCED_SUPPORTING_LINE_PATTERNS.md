- The **`Balanced` label remains fixed**; this note addresses only what may appear **under** it.

- All example wording elsewhere in the exploration stack remains **critique-only**; **nothing in this note is approved final UX copy**, implementation authorization, or product approval.

Under **`Balanced`**, the supporting line should **deepen trust** without implying **more system certainty** than the read-only surface can honestly carry.

## 4. Why a pattern note is needed

The Balanced supporting-line stress test produced enough **line-level signal** to justify moving **beyond individual example lines** toward **reusable pattern rules** scoped to **`Balanced`** only.

The purpose is to preserve:

- **interpretability** gains from light supporting copy
- **honesty** relative to what the primary layer can claim
- **calm** tone and scan-friendly density

while reducing repeated debate over:

- **precision-coded** phrases that read like formal bounds
- **vague** alternatives that trade precision for softness
- **system-authored** language drift in the status block

This note does **not** substitute for governance, disclosure fit, or legal review of any future candidate line.

---

## 5. Core pattern principles

### 5.1 Interpretive, not operational

The line may clarify **posture**; it must not imply **active system action**, runtime actuation, or deterministic state logic.

### 5.2 Calm, not dramatic

The line should improve understanding without alarmism, urgency, defensiveness, or machine-authored narration.

### 5.3 Honest, not precision-coded

The line must not imply **governed ranges**, **thresholds**, or **formal boundaries** unless the surface can genuinely support them.

### 5.4 Plain, not internal

The line should avoid architecture-sounding or model-sounding vocabulary unless deliberately narrowed and retail-natural.

### 5.5 Subordinate, not dominant

The supporting line must remain **secondary** to **`Balanced`** and to the **money anchor** (portfolio value).

## 6. Acceptable pattern types

Patterns below are **acceptable to continue exploring** in critique and Paper tests—not approved copy families.

### 6.1 No supporting line

- Acceptable as a **calm baseline** and **lower-bound control** when judging variants that add words.
- Useful for **regression**: if a supporting line cannot beat **no line** on trust and clarity under honest bounds, it should not advance on wording alone.
- **Weakest on explicit meaning** — highest risk of “atmosphere without consequence” relative to lines that add light interpretability.

### 6.2 Short bounded posture clarification

- Acceptable when it adds **light interpretability** in plain language, **one sentence**, subordinate to the label.
- Should avoid **thresholds**, **guarantees**, **undisclosed triggers**, and any **active-management** or **operational** reading.

### 6.3 Plain-language protection emphasis

- Acceptable only when phrased as **bounded posture**, not **promise** — retail-natural, non-technical, non-advisory.
- Must remain honest to what the read-only layer can support; **translate-before-use** if copy edges toward guarantee or precision-coded reads.

Do not treat any example line in upstream docs as a default ship pattern.

---

## 7. Risky pattern types requiring caution

The following may still be useful in **critique or stress tests** but require **explicit caution** — not casual adoption.

### 7.1 “Protection range” patterns

These patterns often score highest on **retail interpretability** in narrow same-frame tests, but they are **governance-sensitive**: they may imply **formal governed boundaries** or **precision** the current primary layer cannot honestly support without qualification (see guardrails §7.1; vocabulary **must-not-imply** for `Balanced`).

### 7.2 “Position” patterns

Wording that emphasizes **position** or **posture** may **soften threshold-like feel** relative to “range” semantics, but can drift toward **vagueness** or **investment-style** language depending on context and surrounding copy.

### 7.3 Denser explanatory lines

Longer or heavier lines can improve **meaning** but quickly increase **density**, **scan friction**, and **system-authored** tone — closer to **machine-theater** if undisciplined (see micro-test upper-bound signal for Treatment C–class lines).

### 7.4 Future-facing or implied action language

Any line hinting the system is **actively managing**, **responding**, or **changing** allocation in real time is risky unless **separately governed** — it blurs informational posture with operational narrative.

---

## 8. Out-of-bounds patterns

The following are **out of bounds** beneath **`Balanced`** for the **current** Stability Status posture (unless doctrine, disclosure, and sequencing explicitly widen the boundary).

### 8.1 Threshold / trigger patterns

Examples to avoid:

- **threshold** language, **trigger** language, **cutoff** or **score-range** phrasing that implies **governed mechanics** or live rules not established for this layer.

### 8.2 Guarantee-adjacent patterns

Examples to avoid:

- **hard safety** language, **certainty** language, **insurance-like** protection tone, or **outcome-certain** reads at this layer.

### 8.3 Advisory / directive patterns

Examples to avoid:

- telling the user **what to do**
- suggesting **rebalancing**, **moving funds**, **reducing exposure**, or other **obligation** or **guidance** semantics smuggled in as neutral status copy.

### 8.4 Machine-theater patterns

Examples to avoid:

- **internal model narration**
- **autonomous runtime** phrasing
- **engine-like storytelling** as the headline story of the primary status layer

These boundaries keep the status block **interpretive**, not a **visible console** for engine behavior.

---

## 9. Pattern constraints

Practical constraints for supporting lines **beneath `Balanced`**:

- keep to **one short sentence**
- avoid **jargon** and internal architecture nouns unless clearly retail-natural and governed
- avoid **threshold-coded** words and **hidden-engine** vocabulary
- avoid **legal-sounding promises** without review
- avoid **operational verbs** that read like commands or system actions
- keep the line **subordinate** to the label and **portfolio value** (money anchor)

---

## 10. Working pattern signal

**Critique signal only** — not approval, not final vocabulary choice.

- The **strongest recurring pattern** in narrow tests remains a **short bounded line** that improves **interpretability** without exposing machinery.
- **“Protection range”–class meaning** remains the **strongest current interpretability pattern** in the stack and also the **most governance-sensitive** honesty test.
- **Softer / calmer alternatives** may reduce **threshold-like** feel but risk **vagueness** or **investment-adjacent** tone — same-frame critique is required, not assumption.
- **No supporting line** remains a **useful calmness control** and meaningful **lower bound** for comparison.
