## 4. What was tested

The micro-test pressure-tested a small number of upper-shell layout arrangements using the current preferred header-copy direction.

The test focused on:

- balance-first hierarchy
- trust-line placement and subordination
- action-cluster proximity to the money anchor
- transition from header into `Recent activity`

The test did not evaluate:

- broader shell strategy
- CTA redesign
- Family C redesign
- implementation viability
- shipped-route decisions

---

## 5. Variants reviewed

### 5.1 Variant A — Tight money-anchor cluster

Balance remained primary.

Trust remained attached to the balance card.

Actions moved closer to the money anchor while remaining clearly secondary.

`Recent activity` remained the first block below the action cluster.

### 5.2 Variant B — Embedded action adjacency

Actions were placed in a more visibly attached relationship to the balance anchor.

This increased continuity between money and action, but also raised the possibility of action over-promotion.

### 5.3 Variant C — Looser trust separation

Trust was given slightly more breathing room relative to the balance amount.

This tested whether the shell could become calmer without weakening the trust-to-money relationship.

---

## 6. Readout by evaluation criterion

### 6.1 Money-first hierarchy

**Best performer:** Variant A

Variant A preserved the clearest balance-first read.

The amount remained the unquestioned anchor of the upper shell, with trust and action behaving as supporting layers rather than competing elements.

Variant B remained acceptable, but pushed the action layer closer to parity than desired.

Variant C preserved balance-first hierarchy, but introduced a weaker sense of cohesion between the amount and its reassurance layer.

### 6.2 Calm reassurance

**Best performer:** Variant A, with Variant C useful as a calibration check

Variant A kept reassurance present and structurally integrated.

It supported Hedgr’s trust posture without sliding into system narration or detached decorative reassurance.

Variant C offered slightly more breathing room, but risked making trust feel less integral to the money anchor.

Variant B did not directly weaken reassurance language, but its stronger action proximity reduced the felt calmness of the shell.

### 6.3 Action subordination

**Best performer:** Variant A

Variant A produced the strongest balance between availability and restraint.

Actions felt closer to the money anchor, which improved continuity, but they did not become the main invitation of the screen.

Variant B was the clearest overreach detector.

It showed that tighter action attachment can quickly move the product toward a utility-wallet read rather than a calm money-account read.

Variant C kept action pressure lower, but did not improve the money-to-action relationship as effectively as Variant A.

### 6.4 Everyday-money familiarity

**Best performer:** Variant A

Variant A most successfully preserved the sense that the product is about trusted everyday money rather than portfolio tracking or operational status monitoring.

Variant B increased functional immediacy, but at the cost of some premium calmness.

Variant C improved composure slightly, but at risk of weakening the practical unity of money and reassurance.

### 6.5 Subtraction over invention

**Best performer:** Variant A

Variant A improved the shell through tightening and continuity rather than by introducing a new structural idea.

That makes it the strongest fit for the current phase.

Variants B and C were useful as boundary tests, but neither produced a more governed centerline.

---

## 7. Core findings

### 7.1 Strongest current centerline

**Variant A — Tight money-anchor cluster** is the strongest current upper-shell layout direction.

It best supports the existing governed shell while improving the relationship between money, trust, and action.

### 7.2 What the test clarified

- The balance must remain unmistakably first.
- Trust is strongest when it stays closely attached to the money anchor.
- Actions benefit from moving nearer to the balance, but only within a clearly subordinate posture.
- `Recent activity` remains the cleanest transition into the body content.
- The upper shell weakens when action proximity starts to compete with calmness.
- The upper shell also weakens when reassurance becomes too detached from the amount it is helping frame.

### 7.3 What did not change

The test did not produce a reason to reopen:

- the money-first shell
- Family C role
- CTA strategy
- broader visual system direction

The gains remain in precision and restraint, not in structural reinvention.

---

## 8. Risks and boundaries exposed by the micro-test

### 8.1 Action-layer overreach risk

If action controls become too visually attached or too compositionally strong, the shell begins to read more like a utility wallet than a trusted money surface.

### 8.2 Trust-detachment risk

If reassurance is given too much separation from the balance anchor, it can start to feel ornamental rather than integral.

### 8.3 Portfolio-language relapse risk

If future passes combine looser hierarchy with portfolio-coded copy, the shell could drift back toward wealth-tracking posture.

### 8.4 False-progress risk

The micro-test improved local clarity, but it does not by itself constitute design approval, implementation readiness, or shipped-route fitness.

---

## 9. Working recommendation

### 9.1 Adopted paper centerline

Use **Variant A — Tight money-anchor cluster** as the current paper centerline for further critique and refinement.

### 9.2 Action rule

Continue to treat action proximity as an exercise in continuity, not promotion.

### 9.3 Trust rule

Keep reassurance visibly present and attached to the money anchor, but low-pressure in phrasing and visual weight.

### 9.4 Next refinement posture

Any next pass should continue to improve:

- continuity
- familiarity
- calmness
- copy precision

It should not widen into structural redesign unless a later governance artifact identifies a real conflict.

The upper shell is now close enough that the strongest gains continue to come from tightening, not invention.

**the best current header direction keeps the balance unmistakably first, keeps trust closely attached but quiet, and brings actions nearer without allowing them to become the headline.**
