# HEDGR — Class B Jurisdiction Selection Evidence Package

## 1. Status / Authority / Scope

**Status:** Evidence package skeleton; documentation-only.

**Authority:** Non-authoritative evidence-planning artifact subordinate to `docs/ops/HEDGR_STATUS.md`, `AGENTS.md`, accepted ADRs, doctrine, `EVID-B-001`, `EVID-B-002`, `EVID-B-003`, and future qualified legal / compliance review.

**Scope:** Defines required jurisdiction-selection evidence items, source expectations, review questions, blockers, freshness expectations, and downstream dependencies for future Class B consideration.

**Mode:** Evidence package scaffolding only; not evidence gathering, evidence acceptance, jurisdiction selection, legal advice, regulatory approval, or Class B readiness.

This package does not select, approve, recommend, rank, or reject any jurisdiction and does not provide legal advice, claim regulatory approval, approve customer eligibility, approve KYC / AML sufficiency, approve licensing posture, authorize external engagement, approve Class B execution, implementation, staging, live operations, deposits, withdrawals, custody, rails, stablecoin conversion, treasury operations, Copilot execution, or customer fund movement.

## 2. Purpose

This package exists to instantiate the jurisdiction-selection evidence package from the evidence gathering plan and registry.

It answers:

**What jurisdiction-selection evidence must be gathered and reviewed before future Class B ADR drafting readiness or implementation-proposal preflight can be considered?**

It does not answer:

**Which jurisdiction is approved or preferred for Hedgr’s first Class B pilot?**

It does not create:

- jurisdiction approval
- market approval
- operating location approval
- customer location approval
- legal approval
- regulatory approval
- licensing determination
- external legal engagement approval
- implementation authority
- customer-money authority

## 6. Open jurisdiction-selection questions

Answers must come from qualified legal / compliance review and future governed acceptance, not this package.

### Candidate-market framing

- [ ] Which jurisdictions are candidates for future Class B consideration?
- [ ] Are candidates based on founder market intent, user demand, regulatory accessibility, rail availability, operational feasibility, or strategic sequencing?
- [ ] Which candidates are explicitly out of scope for now?
- [ ] Which assumptions are founder assumptions only?

### Operating and user-location facts

- [ ] Where would Hedgr be operating from?
- [ ] Where would users be located?
- [ ] Would the pilot involve one jurisdiction or multiple jurisdictions?
- [ ] Would user location, entity location, rail location, custody location, or treasury location create cross-border obligations?

### Regulatory perimeter

- [ ] What role would Hedgr play in each candidate jurisdiction?
- [ ] Would the activity be regulated?
- [ ] What licensing, registration, exemption, sandbox, partner, or agent pathway may be required?
- [ ] What activity must remain prohibited unless reviewed?

### Customer eligibility

- [ ] Who may participate in each candidate jurisdiction?
- [ ] Are participants individuals, SMEs, internal testers, invited users, employees, or external customers?
- [ ] What residency, sanctions, age, business-type, KYC, transaction-limit, or exposure-limit constraints apply?

### KYC / AML / sanctions

- [ ] What KYC / AML responsibilities apply in each candidate jurisdiction?
- [ ] Who can own KYC / AML controls?
- [ ] What sanctions / PEP screening obligations apply?
- [ ] What evidence must be retained?

### Custody, rails, and stablecoins

- [ ] What custody role is permitted or prohibited in each candidate jurisdiction?
- [ ] What rail / payment permissions are required?
- [ ] What mobile-money, bank, stablecoin, or conversion pathways are legally and operationally feasible?
- [ ] What vendor / partner evidence would be required?

### Data, privacy, support, and complaints

- [ ] What data privacy, residency, and retention constraints apply?
- [ ] What support, complaint, dispute, and incident obligations apply?
- [ ] What user communication requirements apply?
- [ ] What recordkeeping obligations apply?

### Public claims and marketing

- [ ] What claims are prohibited in each candidate jurisdiction?
- [ ] Can Hedgr describe itself as regulated, licensed, approved, wallet, savings, treasury, stablecoin, payment, or yield-related?
- [ ] What disclosures must appear before any customer-money action?

## 11. Downstream dependency effect

- **Legal / compliance evidence package:** jurisdiction selection evidence narrows which legal questions must be reviewed first.
- **Customer eligibility evidence package:** cannot close without jurisdiction-specific eligibility constraints.
- **KYC / AML responsibility evidence package:** depends on jurisdiction-specific control obligations.
- **Custody provider / model evidence package:** must reflect jurisdiction-specific custody role and asset-control constraints.
- **Rail / vendor permission evidence package:** must reflect jurisdiction-specific rail permissions and settlement obligations.
- **Stablecoin / conversion evidence package:** must reflect jurisdiction-specific digital asset, FX, fee, and disclosure constraints.
- **Trust UX / disclosure evidence package:** must avoid claims not supportable in candidate jurisdictions.
- **Pilot Ops evidence package:** must reflect jurisdiction-specific operational, recordkeeping, complaint, and support obligations.
- **Reconciliation / audit evidence packages:** must reflect jurisdiction-specific recordkeeping, retention, and evidence expectations.
- **ADR drafting readiness:** cannot proceed for jurisdiction-sensitive ADRs until jurisdiction evidence exists or blockers are explicitly recorded.
- **Implementation-proposal preflight:** cannot treat jurisdiction questions as closed without accepted evidence.
