# HEDGR - Class B Rail / Vendor Permission Evidence Package

## 1. Status / Authority / Scope

**Status:** Evidence package skeleton; documentation-only.

**Authority:** Non-authoritative evidence-planning artifact subordinate to `docs/ops/HEDGR_STATUS.md`, `AGENTS.md`, accepted ADRs, doctrine, `EVID-B-001`, `EVID-B-002`, `EVID-B-003`, `EVID-B-004`, `EVID-B-005`, `EVID-B-006`, `EVID-B-007`, future qualified legal / compliance review, and future vendor / partner evidence where applicable.

**Scope:** Defines required rail / vendor permission evidence items, source expectations, review questions, blockers, freshness expectations, and downstream dependencies for future Class B consideration.

**Mode:** Evidence package scaffolding only; not evidence gathering, evidence acceptance, rail approval, vendor approval, partner approval, payment-flow approval, integration approval, legal advice, regulatory approval, or Class B readiness.

This package does not approve any rail, vendor, partner, payment provider, mobile-money provider, bank rail, stablecoin rail, deposit path, withdrawal path, conversion path, redemption path, settlement path, rail environment, vendor responsibility model, external engagement, Class B execution, implementation, staging, live operations, deposits, withdrawals, custody, stablecoin conversion, treasury operations, Copilot execution, or customer fund movement.

This package does not provide legal advice or claim regulatory approval.

## 6. Open rail / vendor permission questions

Answers must come from qualified legal / compliance review, future governed acceptance, and vendor / partner evidence where applicable, not this package.

### Rail role and directionality

- [ ] What rail or vendor class is being considered?
- [ ] Is the rail used for deposit, withdrawal, conversion, redemption, settlement, reconciliation, internal testing, or support evidence?
- [ ] Does directionality change legal, custody, reconciliation, support, audit, or user-claim requirements?
- [ ] What rail role is explicitly out of scope?

### Vendor / partner permission

- [ ] What permission is required before using the rail?
- [ ] Is permission for sandbox, internal test, manual pilot, limited live pilot, or production / live?
- [ ] Does public documentation grant permission, or is explicit vendor / partner confirmation required?
- [ ] What permission is required for customer-money exposure?

### Environment-state boundary

- [ ] Is the evidence for mock, prototype, sandbox, internal test, manual pilot candidate, limited live pilot candidate, or production / live?
- [ ] What environment labels are required?
- [ ] What evidence must not be reused across environments?
- [ ] What evidence would be invalid for live customer-money claims?

### Customer-money exposure

- [ ] Does the rail touch customer funds, internal funds, test funds, sandbox balances, vendor test balances, or no funds?
- [ ] Who owns funds during initiation, pending, settlement, failure, reversal, dispute, or unresolved states?
- [ ] What customer-money claims are prohibited without accepted evidence?

### Legal, jurisdiction, and eligibility dependency

- [ ] Which jurisdiction-specific rail permissions apply?
- [ ] Which customer eligibility constraints apply?
- [ ] Which KYC / AML constraints apply before rail use can be assessed?
- [ ] What legal / compliance review is required?

### Custody dependency

- [ ] Which custody boundary does the rail touch?
- [ ] When does custody begin or end relative to the rail event?
- [ ] Who controls assets during rail pending, failure, or dispute states?
- [ ] What custody evidence is required before rail assessment can mature?

### Vendor / partner responsibility

- [ ] What does the vendor or partner own?
- [ ] What remains Hedgr's responsibility?
- [ ] What remains user responsibility?
- [ ] What is the regulated entity role, if applicable?
- [ ] What responsibility evidence is required beyond marketing or public docs?

### Fees, limits, FX, and disclosure dependency

- [ ] What fees, limits, FX rates, spreads, stale-quote risks, and timing constraints may apply?
- [ ] What must be disclosed before user action?
- [ ] What cannot be guaranteed?
- [ ] What evidence is required before any fee / FX / spread claim?

### Failure, support, and incident handling

- [ ] What happens if the rail is delayed, failed, reversed, blocked, duplicated, disputed, unavailable, or unresolved?
- [ ] Who contacts the vendor?
- [ ] Who owns user communication?
- [ ] What support promises are prohibited?
- [ ] What incident path is required?

## 13. Downstream dependency effect

- **Rail settlement / finality evidence package:** cannot mature without rail permission, environment, vendor / partner responsibility, and customer-money boundary evidence.
- **Stablecoin / conversion evidence package:** depends on conversion / redemption permission, custody dependency, fee / FX constraints, and vendor responsibility evidence.
- **Fee / FX / spread evidence package:** must reflect rail-specific fees, limits, quote behavior, stale quote handling, and disclosure constraints.
- **Liquidity / withdrawal control evidence package:** cannot mature without withdrawal rail permission, failure handling, settlement dependencies, and support escalation path.
- **Trust UX / disclosure evidence package:** must reflect rail-specific allowed and prohibited claims.
- **Pilot Ops evidence package:** must reflect rail owner, vendor contact, manual review, escalation, pause, and incident responsibilities.
- **Reconciliation evidence package:** must map rail evidence to user-visible state, support state, custody state, and audit evidence.
- **Support / dispute evidence package:** must define support limits around rail delay, failure, reversal, duplicate events, disputes, outages, and vendor escalation.
- **Audit / retention evidence package:** must capture rail event evidence, permission evidence, vendor evidence, support evidence, and retention requirements.
- **ADR drafting readiness:** cannot proceed for rail-sensitive ADRs until required rail / vendor evidence exists or blockers are explicitly recorded.
- **Implementation-proposal preflight:** cannot treat rail / vendor permission questions as closed without accepted evidence.
