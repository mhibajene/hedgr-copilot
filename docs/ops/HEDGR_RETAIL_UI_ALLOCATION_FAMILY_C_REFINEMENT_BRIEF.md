## 4. Why Family C refinement is needed

Family C emerged as the **strongest current centerline family** in the micro-test and family note—**conditionally**: list clarity plus **light structural cueing**, when calmness and subordination hold.

The unresolved work is now **internal to Family C**:

- how much **structural cue** is enough for grasp vs. how much becomes **busy** or **hierarchy-creeping**
- how to **preserve row clarity** while adding a **bounded** structural read
- how to **avoid drifting** toward **B-like precision** (proportional cues read as measured or live) or **back toward A-like passivity** (trust language without enough structure at a glance)

**Family A** remains the **calm control** and anti-drift baseline. **Family B** remains the **bounded challenger**—useful for pressure-testing grasp, but carrying the **highest precision and chart-adjacent risk** if misapplied. This brief does not equate those roles; it **does not** reopen family comparison as the primary task.

## 5. Refinement objective

**Refine Family C so that list clarity remains primary while structural cueing becomes just strong enough to improve grasp, but never strong enough to imply precision, performance, or hierarchy competition.**

This pass is about:

- **internal cue restraint** and **modest secondary weighting**
- **row/cue balance** (dual encoding without mid-layer overload)
- **subordinate hierarchy** relative to **Portfolio Value** and **Stability Status**
- **calm structural readability**—interpretive structure, not instrument-like reading

This pass is **not** about:

- adding **new visual families** or escaping the A/B/C map
- turning allocation into a **chart**, meter, or scoreboard
- making allocation feel **live**, **ledger-true**, or **operationally exact**
- **redesigning** the broader dashboard, widening IA, or moving the allocation layer

---

## 6. In-scope refinement themes

### 6.1 Row-first clarity

Family C must keep **row clarity primary**: labels and plain-language values anchor the block. The structural cue **reinforces**; it must not **overtake** scan of label/value pairs or push readers into decorative geometry before meaning lands.

### 6.2 Cue restraint

Family C’s cue must stay **secondary**, **modest**, and **clearly interpretive**—suggesting distribution posture, not exact measurement. Any proportional or weighting signal remains **bounded** and **non-heroic**, consistent with guardrails on interpretive vs. exact reads.

### 6.3 Subordinate hierarchy

Family C must remain **visually secondary** to **Portfolio Value** and **Stability Status**. Refinement must not pull **too much attention** into the mid-layer or let allocation **quietly rival** money and status through contrast, shape emphasis, or cue gravity.

### 6.4 Anti-precision discipline

Family C refinement must avoid visual traits that make the split feel **measured**, **live**, **ledger-true**, or **operationally authoritative**—the failure mode most associated with overdressed **Family B**-like proportional reads. Cues stay **structural**, not **performative** or **exact**.

### 6.5 Anti-busyness discipline

Family C refinement must avoid **dual-encoding overload**: list + cue asserting salience together, **clutter**, or **quiet competition** with **Actions** and **Activity**. Mid-layer busyness and accidental emphasis are explicit Family C risks in the readout; this pass screens for them first.

## 7. Translate-before-use themes

Themes that may matter strategically but must **not** be productized directly from this pass.

### 7.1 Stronger structural cues

More assertive cueing **may** improve grasp in some fixtures, but it also raises **precision** and **temperature** risk quickly. Treat as **translate-before-use**: exploratory only; **not** the default refinement direction without guardrail screening.

### 7.2 Richer allocation explanation

Deeper explanatory copy or stacked explanation may matter **later** (e.g. disclosure paths). This pass should **not** “solve” Family C by **adding more copy** on the main surface—the legibility stack prefers **order, weight, and bounded pattern** over density.

### 7.3 Risk signaling inside Family C

Stronger risk signifiers may become relevant under **clearer doctrine** (thresholds, communications rules, legal/trust alignment). They should **not** enter this Family C refinement pass as ad hoc UI without that doctrine—consistent with deferred risk visualization in the broader stack.

## 8. Explicitly deferred

The following remain **deferred** for this bounded Family C refinement pass:

- **Chart-adjacent or meter-like Family C cues** — Deferred: invite precision and performance reads even when static; conflict with calm informational posture and ADR **0013** alignment.
- **Motion-led or animated Family C emphasis** — Deferred: implies liveness and sophistication; pass resolution and micro-test stack defer motion until **static** clarity is exhausted.
- **APY / yield-forward treatment inside Family C** — Deferred: contradicts liquidity-before-yield and non-yield-first doctrine; allocation stays posture/trust, not returns marketing.
- **Protocol-level or engine-level explanation inside the allocation surface** — Deferred: shifts the layer toward engine narration; contradicts read-only informational retail presentation.
- **Broader family reopening beyond A/B/C narrowing** — Deferred: the current task is **internal Family C refinement**; reopening full A/B/C comparison at equal weight would undo the discipline of the family note and guardrails.
