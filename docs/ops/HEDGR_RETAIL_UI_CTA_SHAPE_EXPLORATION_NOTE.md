## 4. Why a CTA shape note is needed

The current shell is now strong enough that CTA shape can be refined without reopening the structural lane.

This note is needed to:

- test whether rounder CTA geometry improves calmness and legibility
- prevent uncontrolled drift into generic fintech or neobank button language
- keep the action layer secondary to money/state
- define the right pressure-test frame before any visual change is treated as useful

This remains governance-safe critique framing only, not implementation direction.

---

## 5. Current CTA signal

- current CTA treatment is workable
- it may still read slightly rigid relative to the calmer money shell
- rounder treatment may improve tactile calmness
- fully pill-shaped treatment is plausible but carries the highest softness and neobank-drift risk

---

## 6. Exploration set

### 6.1 Current control

- baseline reference
- strongest continuity with current expression
- may feel slightly rigid relative to the rest of the shell

### 6.2 Moderately rounded

- likely middle path
- may improve calmness and legibility without over-softening the action layer
- strongest candidate for bounded next-pass testing

### 6.3 Fully pill-shaped

- plausible calmness challenger
- may improve softness and touch clarity
- highest risk of generic fintech or neobank familiarity, or action over-warmth
- must be pressure-tested, not assumed

---

## 7. What Hedgr should preserve in CTA treatment

- clear primary vs secondary action distinction
- calm premium seriousness
- strong legibility
- restrained action energy
- alignment with the money-first shell
- action remaining supportive, not dominant

---

## 8. What Hedgr should avoid in CTA treatment

- overly soft or playful button feel
- generic neobank familiarity
- action warmth overpowering money/state
- pill treatment that feels too consumer-fintech standard
- CTA styling that makes the screen feel less serious
- excessive chromatic or contrast emphasis

---

## 9. Working exploration principles

### 9.1 Calm, not cute

Rounded treatment may soften the action layer, but must not become playful.

### 9.2 Legible, not loud

Shape should improve readability and tap clarity without increasing action dominance.

### 9.3 Premium, not generic

CTA geometry should support Hedgr category identity, not make it feel like a familiar neobank clone.

### 9.4 Supportive, not central

Buttons should remain secondary to the balance anchor and trust context.

### 9.5 Pressure-test the pill

A fully pill-shaped option may be valid, but only if it survives anti-drift scrutiny.

---

## 10. Questions for next-pass CTA testing

- does moderate rounding outperform the current control without losing seriousness?
- does fully pill-shaped treatment improve calmness or merely increase softness?
- at what point does CTA geometry begin to feel neobank-like?
- how should primary/secondary contrast behave if shape softens?
- what CTA traits should be screened before implementation deepens?

This question set is bounded exploration guidance, not implementation direction.

## 12. Working conclusion

CTA shape is now a bounded refinement question inside an otherwise stronger money-first shell. Pill-shaped treatment remains a legitimate challenger only if it preserves seriousness, calmness, and non-neobank identity under explicit anti-drift scrutiny.

**Hedgr’s CTA shape should feel calm and premium enough to support the money shell, but never soft or generic enough to replace the product’s serious posture with familiar fintech friendliness.**
