## 4. Why a fixture brief is needed

The Family C lane is now governed enough to move from abstract refinement notes into fixture-level expression.

This fixture brief is needed to:

- translate Family C guidance into concrete review surfaces
- preserve the cue-range envelope during visual expression
- prevent new fixture work from drifting back into family-level uncertainty
- keep future prototype and Paper passes bounded and comparable

This remains governance guidance for critique only, not implementation direction.

---

## 5. Fixture objective

Express Family C in fixtures so that row-first clarity remains primary, the cue stays interpretive and modest, and the allocation surface feels more structurally legible without becoming more exact, more active, or more assertive.

This pass is about:

- fixture expression of the governed Family C lane
- preserving the current cue envelope
- validating Family C in real review surfaces

It is not about:

- designing a new family
- increasing cue sophistication
- introducing yield-forward or chart-like cues
- redesigning the broader dashboard

---

## 6. What should remain fixed in the fixture pass

The following must remain fixed:

- overall C3 dashboard hierarchy
- Portfolio Value and Stability Status as primary anchors
- allocation informational posture
- allocation labels and trust-surface role unless a separate naming issue is surfaced
- Family C as the active family
- excluded drift traits remain excluded

Changing these would invalidate the pass.

---

## 7. What may vary in the fixture pass

The following may vary, but only within Family C governance boundaries:

- internal cue strength within the governed Family C envelope
- row/cue balance
- modest internal weighting differences
- fixture expression of the accepted cue range
- bounded comparison between C1-informed, C2-centered, and C3-boundary references where useful

All variation remains subordinate to fixed hierarchy, informational posture, and excluded-trait constraints.

---

## 8. What the fixtures should demonstrate

Successful Family C fixtures should make the following visible:

- allocation reads as a coherent trust layer
- structure is graspable without chart behavior
- row-first clarity remains intact
- the cue helps, but does not dominate
- the block remains subordinate to money and status
- the surface feels calmer than B and more structurally helpful than A

---

## 9. What would count as drift in fixture review

The following count as drift and should be treated as out-of-envelope behavior:

- cue begins to look exact or live
- block gains chart-adjacent or instrument-like feel
- visual weight starts competing with Portfolio Value or Stability Status
- internal sophistication rises faster than trust value
- row clarity weakens
- fixture begins to imply operational truth rather than interpretive structure

The Family C lane is ready for fixture-level expression, but only inside the governed cue-range and drift boundaries already established.
