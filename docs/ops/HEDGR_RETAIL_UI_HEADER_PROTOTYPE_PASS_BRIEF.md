## 9. Evaluation questions

Evaluation must remain centered on five governed questions:

- **Money anchor:** Does the balance remain the immediate first read?
- **Trust read:** Does trust read as calm reassurance, attached and subordinate rather than system-led?
- **Action read:** Do actions feel closer and coherent while remaining clearly secondary?
- **Transition read:** Does transition into `Recent activity` remain natural and low-friction?
- **Category read:** Does the upper shell read as trusted everyday money rather than portfolio, utility-wallet, or system-monitoring posture?

---

## 10. Success signals

- balance-first hierarchy remains immediate
- trust reads as reassurance rather than system narration
- trust remains visible but subordinate
- actions feel closer and more coherent without becoming dominant
- `Recent activity` still feels correct
- upper shell feels calmer, clearer, and more human
- no additional structural elements are required
- accepted centerline survives in-context viewing

---

## 11. Failure / reopen signals

Potential reopen signals are present only if evidence repeatedly shows:

- balance no longer anchors first read
- trust repeatedly reads as monitoring, instrumentation, or vague filler
- actions consistently compete with the balance
- `Recent activity` no longer feels natural
- upper shell still reads materially as portfolio-led, utility-wallet-led, or system-led
- accepted centerline cannot hold without adding new structural elements

Failure assessment must be evidence-based.

Taste drift, novelty-seeking, or generic dissatisfaction are not valid reopen grounds.
