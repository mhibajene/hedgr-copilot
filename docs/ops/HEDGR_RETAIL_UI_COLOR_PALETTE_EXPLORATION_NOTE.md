## 1. Purpose

This document defines the current bounded color-palette exploration note for the Hedgr retail UI direction.

Structural and category direction have materially strengthened: the lane favors a money-first shell, calmer compression, and a subordinate trust layer over earn-led or yield-forward framing. The current neutral, money-first shell—deep charcoal / near-black surfaces with restrained hierarchy—is a meaningful step forward for premium calmness and category legibility relative to earlier passes that carried more chromatic accenting.

The goal now is to **formalize palette logic**—how color should support calm money, trust, and legibility—**not** to finalize a brand system, token sheet, or production palette. Nothing in this document is an approved final palette, design-system decision, or implementation authorization.

## 4. Why a palette note is needed

Earlier passes explored multiple chromatic directions and stronger accent postures. The current money-first shell has made a **neutral-first, premium** direction more credible in review: color supports hierarchy instead of competing with it, and the surface reads more serious and trustworthy.

A bounded palette note is needed to:

- formalize the current neutral-first signal without pretending it is final;
- preserve calmness and legibility gains as expression deepens;
- reduce drift toward borrowed app resemblance or neobank-like chromatic excitement;
- begin articulating a **Hedgr-native** calm-money color language.

This remains governance-safe exploration: it does not implement, sequence, or approve production treatment.

---

## 5. Current palette signal

- A **deep neutral / charcoal base** appears stronger than brighter or more stylized alternatives for this lane: it reinforces money-first seriousness and stability-first posture.
- **Premium calmness** is helping **category legibility**—the UI reads more like protected money than like a trader or yield-forward product.
- **Color is supporting hierarchy** rather than competing with Portfolio Value, stability state, and structural order; the trust layer stays interpretive and subordinate (consistent with Family C preserve traits).
- Under the current neutral posture, the **money shell reads more serious and trustworthy**; accent is present but should remain secondary to money and state legibility.

---

## 6. What Hedgr may carry forward

Bounded, non-final admissible carry-forward signals:

- **Deep charcoal / near-black** as a strong base candidate for primary surfaces.
- **Restrained tonal hierarchy**—contrast and spacing doing structural work before hue.
- **Warm-light text on dark neutral surfaces** where readability and premium calm are preserved (warmth ceiling remains a separate refinement question, as in prototype/Paper readouts).
- **Low-noise secondary greys** for supporting labels, metadata, and trust detail.
- **Accent used sparingly and semantically**—guidance, disclosure, or action affordance—not decorative excitement.
- **Palette serving money and state legibility first**; trust layer visibility without hero-like salience.

These are exploration signals for critique and review, not approved tokens or implementation specs.

---

## 7. What Hedgr should avoid

Directions that should remain **risky or inadmissible** for this lane:

- **Over-saturated fintech accenting** or neobank-like chromatic excitement.
- **Trader-like chromatic contrast** or dashboard energy that implies active trading or optimization games.
- **Gain/loss emotional coding** that makes returns feel like the emotional center.
- **Decorative purple (or similar) dependence** without clear semantic role—hue replacing structure.
- **Accent-led hierarchy** where color does more work than typography, spacing, and layer order.
- **Color behavior that makes yield, APY, or “earn” energy more prominent than stability, protection, and access**—including anything that revives earn-first or APY-forward cues ruled inadmissible in fixture and Plasma assessments.

These boundaries align with doctrine, fixture readouts, and spike non-goals; they are not optional styling preferences.

---

## 8. Borrowed reference vs Hedgr-native language

**Stables / Plasma-like** reference work has yielded useful **learnings**: calmer compression, outcome-first readability, and consumer-natural framing without surrendering stability truth—when separated from yield-led or convenience-app identity.

Those learnings must **not** harden into **direct palette dependency**. Resemblance to a specific external app should not become the default Hedgr look; Hedgr now needs its **own** color vocabulary.

**Hedgr-native** color should express **reliability, restraint, and protected-money seriousness** more than convenience-app familiarity or abstracted “friendly money” brightness. Calmness from reference critique is welcome; **palette cloning** is not.

---

## 9. Working palette principles

### 9.1 Neutral-first

Base surfaces should prioritize calm premium neutrality over chromatic personality.

### 9.2 Contrast before hue

Hierarchy should be carried first by contrast, spacing, and typography; hue is a refinement, not the primary grid.

### 9.3 Accent by role, not mood

Accent should support semantics and guidance (actions, disclosure, state emphasis where doctrine allows)—not visual excitement or brand novelty.

### 9.4 Trust over brightness

Palette choices should increase credibility and legibility, not surface energy or hype.

### 9.5 Money first, structure second

The palette should reinforce the shell and governed layer order before it decorates the trust layer; subordination of Family C–style trust detail remains non-negotiable.
