## 4. Why a fixture traits note is needed

Fixture work has now made preserve and avoid rules explicit enough to extract into a durable governance note.

This note is needed to:

- preserve what Family C now does well
- stop future fixture passes from reintroducing already-known drift
- keep fixture review fast, repeatable, and governed
- reduce re-litigation of settled Family C tensions

This remains critique guidance only, not implementation direction.

---

## 5. Traits to preserve

- row-first clarity
- modest secondary cueing
- calm structural assistance
- low visual temperature
- subordinate hierarchy
- interpretive rather than exact read
- trust-surface honesty
- clarity that supports money first, structure second

---

## 6. Traits to avoid

- exact or live-looking cue behavior
- chart-adjacent sophistication
- salience creep
- instrumentation feel
- decorative precision
- over-weighted contrast
- row clarity erosion
- competition with Portfolio Value or Stability Status
- any feeling that allocation is becoming operational truth

---

## 7. Working preserve/avoid signal

- Preserve the traits that keep Family C calmer than B and more structurally useful than A.
- Avoid the traits that make Family C read like a refined instrument instead of a restrained trust layer.
- Use C1-informed expression as a calm floor reference.
- Use C2-centered expression as the main preserve-screen reference.
- Use C3-boundary expression mainly to detect avoid-trait drift.

This is working critique signal only, not approved direction.
